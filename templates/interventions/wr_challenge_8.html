Graded Tasks for Physical Activity

Back to Intervention Access

Let's talk about graded tasks for the promotion of physical activity. Graded tasks begin as easy-to-perform tasks, and become increasingly difficult. For example, you could walk for 100 yards a day for the first week, then half a mile a day for the second week, and then a mile a day for the third week. Using graded tasks is helpful to promote physical activity because it reduces physical activity-related fatigue, pain, stress, and negative emotional attitude.

You are encouraged to practice graded tasks. Please see the instructions.

Physical Activity Intensity Examples

Light-intensity physical activity includes: walking slowly, bicycling slowly, making the bed, preparing food, washing dishes, stretching, dancing slowly, golf-using cart, fishing, housework that involves light scrubbing/cleaning, etc.

Moderate-intensity physical activity includes: walking briskly, yoga, washing windows, carrying a child, shooting a basketball, canoeing, tennis doubles, stair climber with moderate pace, carrying light loads upstairs, housework that involves moderate scrubbing/cleaning, etc.

Vigorous-intensity physical activity includes: running, swimming, shoveling, vigorous dancing, soccer, jumping rope, carrying heavy loads, pushing non-motorized lawnmower, tennis single, stair climber with vigorous pace, carrying heavy loads upstairs, etc.
